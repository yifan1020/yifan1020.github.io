Winifred Galbraith’s English translation of the original poem by Li Bai (BAYAMUS, p.66)

Stefan Themerson used 4 pages (BAYAMUS, pp.69-72) to perform a “Semantic Poetry Translation” of Chinese Tang Dynasty poet Li Bai’s “Drinking under the Moon”. This style of poetry demonstrates a concern for the reality hiding behind language, or, in Themerson’s words, an acute awareness of the existence of facts under the symbolism of labels.

In ancient poetry, some characters contain fixed images, such as “Moon”. “Moon” is a vision imaginable as a meaning, and a natural scene of feeling. The full moon can be compared to the reunion of people, while the waning moon means parting. “Moon” can also symbolize longing:

“床前明月光，疑是地上霜。举头望明月，低头思故乡。”

The moonlight shines before my bed.
I think it must be frost there down,
But then I watch above the moon,
And now I miss afar my town.

Li Bai also extended the moon as a crystal clear realm In “《玉阶怨》” (An Imperial Concubine Waiting at Night), and used the purity of nature to correspond to the purity of people’s hearts, and regarded the moon as the most beautiful and purest symbol.

“玉阶生白露，夜久侵罗袜。却下水精帘，玲珑望秋月。”

Waiting, she finds her silk stockings
soaked with the dew drops
glistening on the marble palace steps.
Finally, she is moving
to let the crystal-woven curtain fall
when she casts one more glance
at the glamorous autumn moon.

The reason why the ancients have a special liking for the “moon” is because the ancients believed that the moon seen in different places is the same, and that the moon can transcend space.

Where does the idiom “Jade Ax Repairing the Moon” come from? How did people in the Tang Dynasty know that the surface of the moon is uneven. The idiom “Jade Ax Repairing the Moon” describes a person who is good at writing articles. “Moon Repairer” is used as a metaphor for a person who is good at writing articles, and “Moon Ax” is used as a metaphor for the ability to write articles.

The allusion of “Jade Ax Repairing the Moon” comes from the chapter《酉阳杂俎》written by Tang Dynasty scholar Duan Chengshi. A man named Wu Gang1, was relegated to the Moon Palace to cut down osmanthus trees because he committed a mistake in learning the art of immortals. Wu Gang cut it with an axe, and the laurel tree would heal immediately. Therefore, Wu Gang could only work endlessly and accept the punishment from heaven.

Before I was 10 years old, I always believed that Wu Gang, Chang’e2 and Jade Rabbit3 lived in the moon. Whenever I saw scattered gray traces in the yellow moon, I would think it was them.

Around the thirty-seventh year of Wanli in the Ming Dynasty (AD 1609), the Italian astronomer Galileo invented the first modern astronomical telescope. He discovered that the surface of the moon is uneven, with craters, depressions, and plains. The real lunar surface was observed for the first time.
