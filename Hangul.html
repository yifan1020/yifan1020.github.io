Non-latin Writing System Study: Hangul

A study of non-Latin writing system. I chose Hangul and studied its history, writing system, and typesetting principles.

The Latin text face is Bembo, the Hangul text face is Sandoll Jeongche 630. Bembo is a serif typeface created by the British branch of the Monotype Corporation in 1928 –1929 and most commonly used for body text. Sandoll Jeongche was initially designed for use in literature. The design objective was to avoid the common flaws seen in Hangul text typefaces and typography while emphasizing clarity and legibility.
