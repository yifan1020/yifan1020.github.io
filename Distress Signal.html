Distress Signal

Signs, symbols, emblems, and signals, in all their diversity, are penetrating and deeply marking expressions of our times, pointing to the future by comprising and conserving something of the past.
— Signs and Symbols, Adrian Frutiger

We use verbal or written language to express the meaning we want to convey. Our language is full of symbols, but we also often use non-strict descriptive symbols or images. Although these have no meaning in themselves, they acquire recognizable meanings through widespread use or intentional use. They are used to represent the objects they are attached to. When a word or an image implies more than its obvious and direct meaning, it becomes symbolic.

For example the wheel may lead our thoughts toward the concept of a “divine” sun; a pointing index finger may index (without referring to) some object in the direction of the line implied by the orientation of the finger, and smoke may index the presence of a fire.

Fire reminds me of danger, signal, and calling for help, so I have this book.
